Early Life:

Cristiano Ronaldo was born in madeira,portugal in 1985,he is named after his father idol Ronald Reagan. Ronaldo has an older brother, Hugo, and two older sisters, Elma and Liliana Cátia "Katia". His mother revealed that, when she was pregnant with Ronaldo, she wanted to have an abortion due to poverty his father's heavy alcoholism and having too many children, but doctors refused to perform the procedure. Ronaldo converted to Catholicism , and was raised in a poor Catholic home, sharing a room with all of his siblings. As a child, Ronaldo played for Andorinha from 1992 to 1995, where his father worked as a kit man ,and then spent two years playing with Nacional . In 1997, aged 12, he had a three-day trial with Sporting CP , who signed him for a fee of £1,500. He then moved from Madeira to Alcochete , a town near Lisbon , to join Sporting's youth academy.By the age of 14, Ronaldo believed he had the ability to play semi-professionally and agreed with his mother to stop his education to focus entirely on football. Although popular with the other students at his school, Ronaldo was expelled after throwing a chair at his teacher, who Ronaldo claimed "disrespected" him. [ 18 ] However, a year later, he was diagnosed with tachycardia , a condition that could force him to stop playing football. [ 19 ] Ronaldo underwent heart surgery in which a laser was used to burn several pathways in his heart into one, changing the way his heart beats when it is at rest. [ 20 ] He was discharged from the hospital a few hours after the operation and resumed football training a few days later. [ 21 ]

Sporting CP :

At the age of 16, Ronaldo was promoted from Sporting's youth team to the first team by manager László Bölöni who was impressed by his dribbling. He then became the first player to play for the club's under-16, under-17 and under-18 teams, the B team and the first team in one season. A year later, on 29 September 2002, Ronaldo made his Primeira Liga debut against Braga and on 7 October, he scored twice as Sporting won 3–0 over Moreirense . During the 2002–03 season,Liverpool manager Gérard Houllier turned down the chance to sign Ronaldo, while manager Arsène Wenger , who was interested in signing Ronaldo, met him at Arsenal's headquarters in November to discuss a possible transfer.
